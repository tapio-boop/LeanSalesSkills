# Lean Sales Method — Core Knowledge

## Foundation

The Lean Sales Method is an end-to-end sales management system based on:
- **Systems thinking**: Sales as interconnected systems, not isolated activities
- **Lean principles**: Flow efficiency, waste elimination, pull-based flow, constraint management
- **Continuous improvement**: Systematic learning loops and process optimization

## Architecture: 26 Agents, 3 Layers

### Strategy Layer (6 agents)
Define WHO to target, WHAT to offer, and HOW to position:
1. **Strategic Market Intelligence Agent** — Market trends, competitive landscape, strategic opportunities
2. **Segment Strategy Agent** — ICPs, segment priorities, target lists
3. **Value Proposition Optimization Agent** — Segment-specific messaging and value props
4. **Offering Configuration Agent** — Offering packages, engagement models, pricing
5. **Channel Strategy Agent** — Channel mix, resource allocation
6. **Pricing Intelligence Agent** — Pricing guidance, competitive pricing

### Management Layer (5 agents)
Optimize flow, quality, and performance:
7. **Sales Operations Management Agent** — Pipeline health, constraint & buffer management, opportunity priority index
8. **Sales Analytics & Forecasting Agent** — Forecasts, performance analytics
9. **Performance Coaching Agent** — Coaching plans, skill development
10. **Quality Assurance Agent** — Quality scores, compliance monitoring
11. **Continuous Improvement Agent** — Process improvements, best practices

### Execution Layer (15 agents)
Execute the sales process:
12-26. Prospect Intelligence, Account Planning, Outreach Automation, Discovery, Qualification, Value Selling, Sales Argumentation, Lead Nurturing, Competitive Intelligence, Proposal Generation, Sales Content, Deal Risk Management, Sales Process Coach, Deal Acceleration, Customer Handoff

## Two Core Systems

### System 1: Opportunity Generation Engine
Transforms strategy into qualified opportunities.
Flow: Strategy → Market Intelligence → Segmentation → Prospect Research → Outreach → Discovery → Qualification

### System 2: Opportunity Pursuit System
Converts qualified opportunities into closed deals.
Flow: Qualified Opportunity → Value Selling → Argumentation → Proposal → Negotiation → Close → Handoff

## Three Workflows

| Workflow | Target | Key Characteristic |
|----------|--------|-------------------|
| **Expand** | Existing customers | Known relationships, highest ROI, account planning focus |
| **Acquire** | New customers in known markets | Competitive displacement, outreach sequences, validated ICP |
| **Enter** | New markets | Hypothesis-driven, lighthouse accounts, validation loops |

## GTM Play — The Unit of Execution

A **GTM Play** binds together:
- **Strategy**: expand, acquire, or enter
- **ICP**: Who to target (company level)
- **Buyer Personas**: Who to engage (individual level, 1:many per ICP)
- **JTBD**: What job each persona hires a solution for
- **Value Proposition**: Why they should buy from us (per persona x JTBD)

## Lean Principles in Sales

- **Flow efficiency**: Minimize wait time between sales activities; measure flow efficiency (value-adding time / total lead time)
- **Pull-based flow**: Only pull new opportunities when capacity exists; the Qualification Agent adjusts thresholds based on pipeline state
- **Constraint management**: Identify and manage the bottleneck in the sales process; the Sales Operations Management Agent monitors constraint utilization
- **Buffer management**: The Lead Nurturing Agent serves as buffer stock, releasing opportunities on pull signals
- **WIP limits**: Limit work-in-progress to prevent overloading the system
- **Waste elimination**: Remove non-value-adding activities (pursuing poor-fit prospects, generic outreach, redundant meetings)
- **Continuous improvement**: Systematic kaizen through the Continuous Improvement Agent
